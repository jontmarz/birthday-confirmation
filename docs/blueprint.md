# **App Name**: Stranger Things RSVP

## Core Features:

- Event Landing Page: Display birthday party details including date (May 23), time (10 AM), location (Salite Mágico amusement park), and the 'Stranger Things' theme, along with an inviting message to celebrate.
- RSVP Form Submission: Provide a form with fields for 'Name' and 'Phone/Whatsapp number' to collect guest attendance confirmations.
- MailerLite Integration: Submit collected guest data (name and phone/Whatsapp) to MailerLite for attendance tracking and management.
- Themed Thank You Page: Upon successful form submission, redirect users to a confirmation page that expresses gratitude and confirms it will be a day of 'pura diversión'.
- AI-Powered Themed Messages: Utilize an AI tool to generate dynamic and personalized 'Stranger Things'-themed messages for the invitation text or the thank you page, adding an extra layer of engagement.

## Style Guidelines:

- Color Anchor: 'Stranger Things' retro-futuristic, eerie atmosphere, 80s neon glow. Scheme: Dark. Primary color: Deep, slightly muted 'Stranger Things' red, contrasting with dark background. (RGB: #B31F1F)
- Background color: Very dark grey with a subtle hint of red, to maintain the eerie ambiance. (RGB: #1A1616)
- Accent color: A vibrant, analogous magenta/fuchsia, reminiscent of 80s neon lights, used for calls-to-action and key highlights. (RGB: #CC3399)
- Headlines: 'Space Grotesk' (sans-serif) for its techy, scientific feel that aligns with the theme. Body text: 'Inter' (sans-serif) for clear readability in a modern context.
- Utilize thematic icons such as small Demogorgon silhouettes, retro pixel art elements, or subtle imagery inspired by 'Stranger Things' props and symbols (e.g., Christmas lights, alphabet wall).
- A single-page, vertically scrolling landing page for clear information flow, with the RSVP form prominently placed. Emphasize responsiveness for both desktop and mobile viewing.
- Subtle background animations like a slow 'VHS static' effect, 'glitch' text on hover for interactive elements, or a flickering neon glow around CTA buttons, evoking the 80s retro aesthetic.
